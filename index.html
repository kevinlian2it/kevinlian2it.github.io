About Me

I’m currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science with a minor in Economics at Columbia University, maintaining a 3.9 GPA and achieving Dean's List honors for four consecutive semesters. My academic focus is on AI and machine learning, areas where I’m passionate about leveraging technology to create innovative, real-world solutions.


In my current role as a Research Assistant at the Sustaining Peace Project at Columbia Climate School, I’m exploring the intersection of AI, natural language processing, and peace studies. This work involves analyzing vast datasets using NLP techniques and building a high-performance vector database for large language models, helping enhance research into sustainable peace. Previously, I interned at Yuzhi Technologies in Beijing, where I optimized ChatGPT prompts and performed data-driven market research, contributing to the successful launch of a health app with over 4,000 users within its first week.


Apart from my technical pursuits, I have a strong interest in Economics, which I study as a minor. This adds a valuable perspective to my work, especially in data analysis, as I consider economic impacts alongside technical solutions. Outside of academia and work, I’m an avid soccer fan and enjoy exploring diverse cuisines, always on the lookout for new flavors and experiences.
